The Ulam Spiral

Lets face it, primes are some of the most fascinating numbers out there. In 1963 a mathematician by the name Stanislaw Ulam was very bored at a scientific meeting and doodling in his notebook, he noticed that starting from the center with 1 and working counter clockwise out writing the next number along the edges of squares, primes had a strong tendency to appear on diagonals. You can read more about it on wikipedia, but here is a Ulam Spiral, the black dots are primes, red is the center. Be careful with max primes, at the max (500000) it can crash some computers.
